Why a Single Score Is Not Sufficient

A professionally defensible DMA cannot rest on a single subjective score per topic. Each material topic must be decomposed into multiple targeted questions covering Impact Materiality and Financial Materiality independently. Structured multi-question scoring eliminates individual bias and produces an objective, audit-ready evidence trail.

Mitigates Bias
Specific dimensional questions prevent participants from defaulting to a single gut-feeling response across all topics.
Ensures Granularity
Separate questions capture critical nuance, such as the distinction between immediate operational disruption and long-term reputational damage.
Audit Defensibility
A structured trail of scored questions provides the evidence base required when a sustainability auditor challenges your materiality conclusions.
Strategic Clarity
Multiple questions reveal precisely where a risk originates, enabling sharper management decisions on whether the source is regulatory exposure, market share, or reputational risk.
For this tool, each stakeholder provides a single consolidated score per topic per dimension. In a full professional assessment, replace these scores with averaged responses from your structured engagement questionnaire.
